History of Bengaluru

Bengaluru, formerly known as Bangalore, has a rich and vibrant history that dates back to the 16th century. It was originally a small settlement ruled by local chieftains. In the 17th century, it came under the rule of the Mysore Kingdom, which transformed it into a major center of trade and commerce.

In the 19th century, Bengaluru became a British military garrison and eventually grew into a large city. It served as the capital of the Mysore Princely State until India's independence in 1947. After independence, Bengaluru emerged as a major industrial and technological hub, earning it the nickname "Silicon Valley of India."

Timeline of Important Events

  • 1537: Kempe Gowda I, a chieftain, founds Bengaluru.
